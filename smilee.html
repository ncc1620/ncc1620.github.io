About

SmiLee is the long‑standing multi‑disciplinary creative persona of Lee Richard Grimwade — an improvisational, proximity‑based, community‑centered identity active since at least 1998. The persona spans documentary practice, live music and performance, photography, mythic storytelling, and symbolic worldbuilding. SmiLee weaves together concert films, artist stories, symbolic frameworks, and community‑rooted creative practice into a living archive of culture and connection.

Within the YOU ARE HERE creative ecosystem, SmiLee functions as the expressive, performative, and mythic layer of the work — a living interface between documentary presence, improvisational performance, and long‑form worldbuilding. The persona appears across field recordings, live‑event documentation, experimental sound work, and collaborative projects that foreground artists, communities, and place‑based culture.

Historical Provenance

The persona “SmiLee” has been in continuous use since the late 1990s. One of the earliest public appearances of the name is the independently hosted website smileeonline.com, archived in 2001. This early web presence predates modern social media and establishes the persona’s long‑standing creative lineage.

A later public documentation of the persona appears in the Beards.org Switzerland gallery (January 25, 2006), where “SmiLee Grimwade” is featured as part of an international beard photography collection. This provides a second timestamped, third‑party record of the persona’s existence and activity.

Additional third‑party references include a 2020s Facebook post by Patrick R. Cleary crediting “SmiLee Kalyāṇamitratā von Grimmigwaten” for photography work, further demonstrating the persona’s continued use across decades.
