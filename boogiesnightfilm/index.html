Boogie’s Night (2000)

A Hi8 Underground Documentary by SmiLee Grimwade

Overview

Boogie’s Night is a vérité‑style underground documentary shot on Hi8 analog video during a chaotic house‑party night in Küsnacht, Switzerland (2000). The film captures the raw, handheld, late‑90s aesthetic of youth culture, improvised performances, and the unpredictable energy of the Zürich underground scene.

Two versions were completed in 2000:

  • Official Release — 79 minutes
  • Director’s Cut — 120 minutes

Both versions are now considered lost media.

Synopsis

Shot on Hi8 and assembled on VHS decks, Boogie’s Night documents a single night inside Zürich’s youth underground. The film follows the music, chaos, and improvised performances that unfolded in a cramped Küsnacht apartment, culminating in a spontaneous striptease by a heavily intoxicated Latino male with long curly dark hair — captured as part of the film’s vérité documentation.
